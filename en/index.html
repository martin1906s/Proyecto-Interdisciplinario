INTRODUCTION

In a world where the quality of the air we breathe directly influences our health and the sustainability of the planet, research on air pollutants is more relevant than ever. Substances such as suspended particles, nitrogen oxides, sulfur dioxide, carbon monoxide, volatile organic compounds and greenhouse gases have a significant impact on the environment and on living beings. These pollutants not only aggravate global challenges such as climate change and the deterioration of the ozone layer, but also affect public health, causing respiratory and cardiovascular diseases and allergies. At an environmental level, pollutants compromise the vitality of ecosystems, damage vegetation, contaminate bodies of water and threaten biodiversity. Faced with this problem, it is essential to promote effective strategies to mitigate their emissions and promote sustainable practices that contribute to a cleaner and more balanced future. Discover with us how to face these challenges and build a healthier environment for all.
