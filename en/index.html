INTRODUCTION

Investigating the presence of air pollutants such as suspended particles, nitrogen oxides, sulfur dioxide, carbon monoxide, volatile organic compounds and other greenhouse gases is essential to understanding their impact on the environment, humans and animals. These pollutants not only contribute to global problems such as climate change and the deterioration of the ozone layer, but are also responsible for the degradation of air quality, causing respiratory and cardiovascular diseases and allergies in people. In addition, they negatively affect ecosystems, damaging vegetation, water bodies and fauna, putting biodiversity at risk. It is crucial to develop strategies to mitigate their emission and promote sustainable practices that reduce their presence in the atmosphere.
